Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Need to Know Before Booking

What can counselling help with?
+
Counselling supports a wide range of emotional, psychological, relational, and life challenges — including anxiety, depression, trauma, grief, burnout, relationship difficulties, low self-esteem, life transitions, cultural adjustment, and personal growth. Many people also find value in having a confidential space simply to process daily stress and understand themselves better.
How do I know if counselling is right for me?
+
You don't need to be in crisis to benefit from counselling. If you're feeling stuck, overwhelmed, disconnected, or simply want to understand yourself better — counselling can help. Our free 15-minute consultation is a relaxed, no-pressure way to explore whether Ginhawa is the right fit for you.
How long does counselling take?
+
There's no fixed timeline. Some people benefit from 6–12 focused sessions; others prefer longer-term ongoing support. The duration depends on your goals, the nature of your concerns, and how you respond to the process. We review progress regularly and always respect your pace.
Is everything I share confidential?
+
Yes. Confidentiality is fundamental. Everything you share is protected by professional ethical standards and Canadian privacy law (PIPEDA). Your counsellor will explain the limited legal exceptions — such as imminent risk of harm — during your first session.
Do you offer virtual/online sessions?
+
Yes — we offer secure, encrypted video sessions for clients anywhere in Canada. Online sessions are as effective as in-person and provide greater flexibility. Many clients find the comfort of their own space actually enhances the therapeutic work.
What happens during the first session?
+
Your first session is a relaxed, collaborative conversation. Your counsellor will introduce themselves, explain the process, and gently explore what brings you in. You control what you share and when. By the end, you'll have a clearer picture of what working together might look like.
Do you provide culturally informed care for Filipino-Canadians?
+
Yes — deeply so. For Filipino-Canadian clients, this means understanding family dynamics, intergenerational expectations, immigrant experience, language and identity, and the specific pressures of navigating Canadian life while honouring cultural roots. You never have to translate yourself here.
How do I pay? Does insurance cover counselling?
+
We accept e-transfer and major credit cards. Many Canadian extended health benefit plans cover counselling with a Registered Psychotherapist (RP) or Registered Clinical Counsellor (RCC). We recommend checking your plan directly. Contact us with any billing questions — we're happy to help clarify your options.
