Understanding Anxiety

When Worry Takes Over Your Life

Anxiety can feel like a constant state of bracing for something to go wrong. It can show up as racing thoughts, physical tension, avoidance, or a sense of dread that never fully lifts. Anxiety is one of the most common experiences people seek counselling for — and it is also one of the most responsive to therapy.

Common Symptoms:

  • Persistent worry and racing thoughts
  • Physical tension, chest tightness, or shortness of breath
  • Panic attacks or intense episodes of fear
  • Social anxiety and difficulty in group settings
  • Avoidance of situations that feel threatening
  • Sleep difficulties and restlessness
  • Irritability, fatigue, and difficulty concentrating

Treatment Approaches

How We Help With Anxiety

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)

Identify and shift the thought patterns fuelling your anxiety — replacing unhelpful thinking with more balanced perspectives.

Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT)

Learn to hold anxiety with less struggle — making room for difficult feelings without letting them run your life.

Mindfulness-Based Techniques

Develop present-moment awareness to reduce reactivity and build a calmer, more grounded relationship with your thoughts and feelings.

FAQs

Anxiety Counselling Questions

Can counselling really help with anxiety?
+
Yes — anxiety is one of the most well-researched areas in psychotherapy and responds very well to evidence-based approaches like CBT and ACT. Most clients notice meaningful improvement within a few months of consistent therapy.
Do I need medication alongside counselling?
+
Not necessarily. Many people find counselling alone highly effective. If medication may be helpful, your counsellor can discuss this with you and support you in consulting a physician if appropriate.
How soon will I feel better?
+
Everyone's experience is different. Many clients notice shifts within the first few sessions. Lasting change typically develops over weeks to months, depending on the nature and duration of anxiety.
