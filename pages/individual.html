What is Individual Counselling?

You Don't Have to Navigate This Alone

Individual counselling gives you a private, judgment-free space to talk openly about what's going on in your life. Whether you're dealing with anxiety, grief, burnout, relationship difficulties, or simply a sense that something isn't right — your counsellor is here to listen, reflect, and support you.

Common Concerns We Support:

  • Anxiety, worry, and panic attacks
  • Low mood, depression, and emotional numbness
  • Trauma and difficult past experiences
  • Low self-esteem and negative self-talk
  • Work stress and burnout
  • Life transitions and identity questions
  • Relationship and communication difficulties
  • Cultural adjustment and immigration stress

Benefits

What You Can Gain From Individual Counselling

🧠 Clarity & Self-Understanding

Gain insight into patterns, emotions, and beliefs that may be keeping you stuck — and understand where they come from.

🛠 Practical Coping Tools

Learn evidence-based strategies to manage anxiety, low mood, stress, and emotional overwhelm in everyday life.

💬 A Safe Space to Be Heard

Experience the relief of speaking openly without fear of judgment — with someone who is genuinely present with you.

🌱 Personal Growth

Build self-compassion, confidence, and a clearer sense of your values — becoming more of who you want to be.

🔗 Better Relationships

As you understand yourself better, your relationships with others often deepen and become more fulfilling.

🌿 Lasting Resilience

Develop the inner resources to navigate future challenges with greater ease, perspective, and self-trust.

How It Works

The Counselling Process

1

Free 15-Minute Consultation

We start with a relaxed introductory call so you can ask questions, share what you're looking for, and get a feel for whether Ginhawa is the right fit.

Initial Assessment Session

Your first full session explores your background, concerns, and goals. We collaboratively create a plan that honours your priorities and pace.

FAQs

Individual Counselling Questions

How long does individual counselling take?
+
There's no set timeline. Some clients benefit from 6–12 focused sessions; others prefer longer-term ongoing support. We review progress regularly and always follow your lead.
Can I do sessions online?
+
Yes — we offer secure video sessions for clients anywhere in Canada. Many clients find the comfort of their own home actually enhances the therapeutic process.
What if I've never been to counselling before?
+
That's completely okay. Many of our clients are coming to counselling for the first time. Your counsellor will guide you gently through the process — there's no pressure to share more than you're comfortable with.
Is everything I share kept confidential?
+
Yes. Confidentiality is fundamental to our practice. Your counsellor will explain the limited legal exceptions — such as imminent risk of harm — during your first session.
