Understanding Trauma

Trauma Lives in the Body and in Memory

Trauma is not just what happened — it is what happened inside you as a result. It can show up as flashbacks, hypervigilance, emotional numbness, difficulty trusting others, or a sense of being permanently changed by your experiences. These are normal responses to abnormal events.

Our Trauma-Informed Principles:

  • Safety first — always, in every session
  • Your pace, your choice, your control
  • Stabilization before processing
  • Integrating body and mind in healing
  • Honouring resilience alongside wounding
  • Cultural sensitivity in understanding trauma
  • No pressure to relive what you're not ready for

Safe Healing

What Trauma Recovery Looks Like

Phase 1: Safety & Stabilization

Before processing trauma, we build a foundation of safety — coping tools, grounding skills, and a trusting therapeutic relationship.

Phase 2: Processing

When you're ready, we work with trauma memories at your pace — using evidence-based approaches that reduce their emotional charge.

Phase 3: Integration & Growth

Reconnecting with your life, your relationships, and your sense of identity — moving forward with greater wholeness and resilience.

FAQs

Trauma Counselling Questions

Do I have to talk about my trauma in detail?
+
No — not immediately, and never in a way you're not ready for. Trauma-informed therapy prioritizes your sense of safety and agency above all else. We move at your pace, always.
What types of trauma do you support?
+
We support clients with many forms of trauma — including childhood trauma, relationship trauma, cultural and intergenerational trauma, immigration-related trauma, loss, and experiences of discrimination or violence.
