Depression is more than sadness. It is a heaviness that touches everything. Recovery is possible — and you don't have to travel that path alone.

What Depression Can Feel Like

More Than Just Sadness

Depression can drain your motivation, isolate you from people you love, and make even small tasks feel impossible. It often comes with a deep sense of hopelessness — a belief that things won't get better. That feeling is a symptom, not a fact. With support, things can and do change.

Common Symptoms:

  • Persistent low mood or emotional flatness
  • Loss of interest in things once enjoyed
  • Fatigue and low energy despite rest
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Changes in sleep or appetite
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Social withdrawal and isolation

FAQs

Depression Counselling Questions

Is counselling effective for depression?
+
Yes. Multiple forms of therapy — including CBT, ACT, and interpersonal therapy — have strong evidence for treating depression. For mild to moderate depression, therapy is often as effective as medication. For severe depression, a combined approach may be recommended.
What if I don't have the energy to engage?
+
This is completely understood. Depression itself makes taking action feel impossible. We meet you exactly where you are — there are no expectations around how 'well' you show up to a session. Just arriving is enough to begin.
